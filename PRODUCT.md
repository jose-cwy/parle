# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Teens and young adults navigating heartbreak, loneliness, or emotional overwhelm after a breakup. They use Heartstrings Club in private moments — often late at night, on their phone or laptop, when they need a calm space to write, reflect, or feel supported without judgment.

## Product Purpose

Heartstrings Club is a private digital sanctuary for heartbreak support. It offers letter writing (to your future self), a private diary with calendar, an emotionally aware AI chat companion, and a chaptered quotes library. Success means users feel safe enough to return regularly, express difficult feelings, and notice small signs of healing over time.

## Brand Personality

Warm, gentle, and honest. Like a trusted friend who listens without fixing. Voice is soft and direct — never clinical, never preachy, never performatively cheerful.

**Three words:** cozy, private, supportive

**Emotional goals:** calm, safety, quiet hope

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages (hero metrics, identical card grids, gradient text)
- Cold clinical mental-health UI (sterile white, stock photos, corporate blue)
- Neon cyberpunk or high-contrast "AI tool" aesthetics
- Glassmorphism-as-decoration, side-stripe accent borders, modal-first flows
- Loud, overstimulating gamification or social-feed patterns
- Copy that sounds like a therapist brochure or a motivational poster

## Design Principles

1. **Sanctuary over spectacle** — The interface should feel like a room you want to stay in, not a product demo.
2. **Privacy is visible** — Users should feel their words are held carefully; avoid social-proof pressure or public metrics.
3. **Motion with weight** — Animations are fluid and calm, like golden-hour light, never snappy or mechanical.
4. **Warmth without infantilizing** — Supportive and soft, but respectful of teen intelligence.
5. **Show the craft** — Hand-drawn illustration, paper textures, and organic shapes signal human care.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for body text and interactive elements
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all looping animations
- Do not rely on color alone for state (use labels, borders, or icons)
- Support keyboard focus states on all clickable elements
- Use plain, readable language; avoid idioms that assume specific cultural context
